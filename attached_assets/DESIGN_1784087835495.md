## Brand & Style

The design system is rooted in the "Quiet Luxury" ethos—an aesthetic defined by understated elegance, superior quality, and a rejection of logomania. It targets a high-discerning audience that values exclusivity and craftsmanship. 

The design style is a blend of **High-End Minimalism** and **Editorial Design**. It prioritizes generous whitespace (breathing room), intentional asymmetrical layouts, and a sophisticated hierarchy that allows high-fashion photography to serve as the primary visual anchor. The emotional response should be one of calm, confidence, and timelessness. 

Key visual principles:
- **Spatial Discipline:** Every element has a reason for existing; if it doesn't add value, it is removed.
- **Editorial Flow:** Layouts should feel like a premium print magazine, utilizing "white space" as a functional design element rather than "empty" space.
- **Micro-interactions:** Transitions should be slow, eased, and purposeful, mimicking the graceful movement of high-quality fabric.

## Colors

The palette is restrained and tonal, leaning on high contrast between light and dark to create a sense of drama and focus.

- **Primary (Deep Charcoal):** Used for primary typography, icons, and high-impact structural elements. It provides the "weight" and authority.
- **Secondary (Soft Champagne):** Used for subtle backgrounds, sectional dividers, or hover states to soften the transition between ivory and charcoal.
- **Tertiary (Subtle Gold):** Reserved exclusively for small accents, such as price points, active states, or premium badges. It must be used sparingly to maintain its value.
- **Neutral (Ivory):** The primary canvas color. It is warmer than pure white, providing a more "expensive" and organic feel to the screen.

## Typography

Typography in this design system is the primary vehicle for the brand's personality. 

**Playfair Display** (Headings) provides the editorial, high-fashion soul. It should be set with tight tracking in larger sizes to emphasize its elegant serifs.

**Montserrat** (Body & Nav) acts as the modern, functional counterpoint. To achieve the "luxury" look, body text and navigation items utilize wide letter-spacing (tracking) and lighter font weights (300-400). This increases legibility and creates an airy, sophisticated feel.

**Hierarchy Rules:**
- Use **Display XL** for hero sections only, often overlapping or interacting with photography.
- **Navigation Links** and **Labels** must always be uppercase with high tracking (minimum 0.15em) to differentiate them from standard body text.

## Layout & Spacing

The layout philosophy is a **Fixed-Fluid Hybrid**. While the central content container has a maximum width to ensure readability and photographic impact, the negative space on the flanks should feel infinite.

- **The 12-Column Grid:** A standard 12-column grid is used, but elements should frequently "break" the grid or span unusual column counts (e.g., a product image spanning 7 columns with 5 columns of whitespace) to create an editorial, non-templated look.
- **Vertical Rhythm:** We use large vertical spacing (`stack-xl`) between major sections to emphasize the exclusivity of the content. 
- **Mobile Adaptation:** On mobile, margins reduce significantly, and typography scales down, but the vertical "breathability" remains high to prevent the UI from feeling cramped.

## Elevation & Depth

This design system avoids traditional shadows to maintain a flat, modernist aesthetic. Depth is achieved through **Tonal Layering** and **Line Work**.

- **Flat Stack:** Elements are positioned directly on the Ivory (#FFFFFF) or Champagne (#F7E7CE) base. 
- **Thin Outlines:** Instead of shadows, use 0.5pt or 1pt solid borders in Charcoal (#1A1A1A) with low opacity (10-20%) to define boundaries of cards or inputs.
- **Z-Index Overlays:** Overlapping elements (e.g., text partially covering an image) create a physical sense of depth without needing artificial drop shadows.
- **Glassmorphism (Special Use):** For sticky navigation bars only, use a heavy backdrop blur (20px+) with a 90% opaque Ivory background to maintain a sense of place as the user scrolls.

## Shapes

The shape language is strictly **Sharp (0px)**. 

Luxury is often associated with architectural precision. All buttons, image containers, input fields, and structural dividers must use hard 90-degree corners. This evokes a sense of custom tailoring and structural integrity. 

- **Exception:** Small functional elements like radio buttons may remain circular for usability, but all primary UI containers must remain rectangular.

## Components

### Buttons
- **Primary:** Solid Charcoal (#1A1A1A) background, Ivory (#FFFFFF) text, sharp corners. High tracking on uppercase Montserrat text.
- **Secondary:** Transparent background, 1px Charcoal border, sharp corners.
- **Ghost:** No border, underlined text (1px offset) that disappears on hover.

### Input Fields
- Underline style only: A 1px Charcoal border on the bottom. No side or top borders. Labels float above in `label-sm` typography.

### Cards (Product)
- No borders or shadows. The image takes 100% width. Typography (Product Name and Price) is centered underneath with significant padding. Prices are highlighted in Subtle Gold (#C5A059).

### Navigation
- A centered or split-logo top bar. Hover states for links involve a slow fade or a thin 1px underline transition.

### Lists
- Separated by thin (0.5px), full-width horizontal rules in Charcoal at 10% opacity. This mimics the look of a luxury invoice or ledger.
